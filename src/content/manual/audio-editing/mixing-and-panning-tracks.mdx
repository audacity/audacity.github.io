## Using the Track Controls

You can adjust volume and panning per track on the left-hand side of any track:

![](https://support.audacityteam.org/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F2905281570-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FEnNXGrLkN0pXlkzzS7cM%252Fuploads%252FBbpLbUQRw20zGMNVhk2y%252FAudacity4_zTl9lkzcHF.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D96e2305b-2e2b-487c-978c-4216efc10423&width=768&dpr=3&quality=100&sign=547d029e&sv=2)

The Track Control Panel features a Volume Slider (+ to -) and a Panning Slider (L to R)

<Callout> 
If you collapse a track or make it vertically smaller, Audacity will hide the pan and volume sliders from view. To see them again, expand the track by dragging down on it's lower edge.
</Callout>
