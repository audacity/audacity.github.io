# Toolbars Overview

Audacity 4 has three main toolbars:

1. **Project Toolbar** — docked at the very top of the window. Holds Home/Project/Export navigation, quick actions (Mixer, Audio setup, Share audio, Get effects), the workspace selector, and Undo/Redo.
2. **Transport Toolbar** — your main working toolbar. Holds playback and recording controls, editing tools, zoom, snapping, timecode, and the master meter. This is the one toolbar you can move.
3. **Selection Toolbar** — docked at the bottom of the window. Shows your current selection's start, end, and duration.

## Workspaces

A **workspace** selector in the Project Toolbar lets you choose between **Classic**, **Modern**, **Music**, and **Spectral editing** layouts. Switching workspaces changes which tools appear in the Transport Toolbar — for example, the Music workspace adds tempo and time-signature controls, while Spectral editing simplifies the toolbar down to just zoom and a waveform/spectrogram switch. Pick whichever workspace matches what you're working on.

## Moving the Transport Toolbar

Grab the Transport Toolbar's drag handle and drag it:

- Drop it near the top of the window to dock it at the top.
- Drop it near the bottom of the window to dock it at the bottom.
- Drop it anywhere else to leave it floating at that position.

The Project Toolbar and Selection Toolbar stay fixed in place.

## Navigating toolbars with the keyboard

Once a toolbar has focus, arrow keys move between its buttons and controls. See Accessibility and Keyboard Navigation for the full keyboard navigation model, including an alternative mode for screen reader users.
